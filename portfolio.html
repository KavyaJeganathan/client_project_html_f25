Portfolio Support

Create A Portfolio That Showcases Your Work

Your portfolio showcases who you are as a professional. It’s more than a website. It’s your chance to show your process, creativity, and the real-world impact of your work.

While your resume tells employers what you’ve done, your portfolio shows them how you work. A strong portfolio helps employers assess your skills, your thinking, and whether you’re a good fit for their team or project. Portfolios are typically required for UX Design roles but can also benefit students across all pathways.

  • Start building your portfolio over winter break in year one (if possible)

What's in a Strong Portfolio?

Most portfolios include 3-5 projects and feature:

  • Home Page: Clear, minimal, and branded intro with links to key projects.
    • Focus on linking to 3-5 standout projects.
    • Use concise text, clear structure, and consistent branding.
    • Include a tagline that reflects your branding and design approach.
  • About Me: Tell your story in 2–3 paragraphs.
    • Show your personality and journey.
    • Add a high-quality headshot.
    • Keep it to 2–3 short paragraphs.
  • 3–5 Case Studies: For each project, include:
    • Summary including your role, tools, timeline.
    • Problem statement including project context, and goals.
    • Process including research, ideation, and testing.
    • Final designs and outcomes.
    • Key takeaways or lessons learned.
  • Links To Include: Resume (PDF), LinkedIn, GitHub (if applicable)
  • Optional Other Artifacts: Art, design, or additional work

Portfolio Best Practices

  • Use templates, no need to code from scratch.
  • Use clear navigation, especially on mobile and tablet.
  • Add alt text for images.
  • Keep pages clean, scannable, and visual.
  • Customize, make it reflect your personal brand.
  • Proofread everything, spelling and grammar count.
  • Explain UMSI-specific terms using industry language.

If You're Stuck On Project Ideas Due To:

Not Having Work Experience

  • Use course projects, design sprints, or student org work.
  • Start a personal project (e.g. redesign an app or physical product).
  • Join daily UX/UI challenges.
  • Focus on process and learnings even if a project isn’t finished.

Working Under NDAs

  • Use generalized language.
  • Modify or recreate key visuals.
  • Highlight your role and learnings.
  • See how others do it:
    • Vectorform – UMSI Alum
    • Meta Internship – UMSI Alum

Final Tips

  • Keep your portfolio updated, it is a living document.
  • Focus on storytelling and visual process.
  • Proof-read every page of your portfolio.
  • Link to your resume, LinkedIn, and GitHub if relevant.
  • Ask a peer or coach to test your portfolio and ask questions like: "can this portfolio be navigated in under 2 minutes?"

Portfolio FAQs

  • Q: How many projects should I include?
    A: 3–5 solid case studies is ideal. Quality > quantity.
  • Q: What if I don’t have internship experience?
    A: Use coursework, personal projects, and student org work. Employers value process.
  • Q: Can I use Wix, Squarespace, or Notion?
    A: Yes, templates are totally fine! Customize it for branding and clarity.
  • Q: What if a project isn’t finished yet?
    A: Only publish what's ready. Unpublish or outline next steps if incomplete.
