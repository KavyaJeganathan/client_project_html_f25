Craft a Resume that Tells Your Story

Your resume is often your first impression—an opportunity to highlight your unique strengths, skills, and experiences to potential employers. Whether you’re starting from scratch or looking to polish an existing resume, these resources are here to help you every step of the way.

UMSI students are in high demand, and a clear, well-organized resume will help you stand out among applicants. On this page, you'll find practical tips, templates, and guides—all tailored to the needs of School of Information students and aligned with employer expectations.

Need feedback? You can always meet with a CDO Career Coach to review your materials.

Resume Writing Made Easy: Steps to Success

  1. Download a UMSI-Approved Template: Start with a format that works—no need to design from scratch.
  2. Gather Your Experiences: List jobs, internships, volunteer roles, coursework, and projects most relevant to your goals.
  3. Write Impactful Bullets: Use action verbs and highlight your skills, results, and unique contributions.
  4. Tailor for Each Opportunity: Customize your resume for every job or internship you pursue.
  5. Review and Edit: Proofread for errors, check formatting, and ask for feedback from peers or a career coach.

Resume Writing Best Practices: Tips & Tricks

Have both a master and tailored resume:

  • Master Resume: Comprehensive resume, all experiences and skills listed. For your reference only; no page limit.
  • Tailored Resume: Customized resume for each job application. Focus on relevant skills, keywords, and recent experience.

Have a clear structure and formatting:

  • Use clear, professional fonts (e.g., Arial, Times New Roman) at 10pt minimum.
  • Limit to two font types max.
  • Keep resumes to one page, two if necessary.
  • Consistent formatting for headings, dates, bullets.
  • Avoid headers for contact info on page one.

Include key resume sections:

  • Contact Info: Name, phone, email at top. Include LinkedIn, portfolio, GitHub links. Omit full address for privacy.
  • Education: List most recent degree first, with official titles and dates. Add relevant coursework or projects if needed.
  • Experience: List in reverse chronological order. Use strong action verbs. Write bullets with A-C-R method. Use past tense for previous roles, present tense for current.
  • Projects: Separate section if needed, formatted like experience.
  • Leadership: Include relevant extracurriculars and roles.
  • Skills: Technical, language, or methodological skills grouped with commas. Include proficiency if applicable.

Include optional sections if desired:

  • Honors & Awards
  • Publications & Presentations
  • Certifications & Training
  • Volunteer Experience

Optimizing Your Resume

Write Strong Resume Bullets: The A-C-R Method

  • Action: What you did
  • Context: How or why you did it
  • Result: Outcome or impact

Example: Collaborated with Special Collections Manager to update SOPs, improving workflow consistency.
Avoid vague or passive phrases like “responsible for” or “supported.”

Optimizing for Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS)

  • Tailor resumes with keywords from job postings.
  • Use common section headers (Education, Experience, Skills).
  • Avoid tables, headers, footers, images, colors.
  • Write out months and years fully (e.g., September 2023).
  • Use bullet points, not dashes.
  • Submit PDFs converted from Word or Google Docs.

Using AI Tools

AI tools like ChatGPT and Grammarly are helpful for brainstorming and drafting. Keep these tips in mind:

  • Personalize all AI-generated content—never copy/paste as is.
  • Edit carefully for accuracy and authenticity.
  • Avoid uploading sensitive info.
  • Use AI to tailor resumes by comparing job descriptions and identifying keywords.

Final Tips

  • Use tools like Resume Worded to check ATS compatibility.
  • Keep resumes between 450-600 words for best results.
  • Maintain professional social media profiles and include LinkedIn URLs.

Resume FAQs

  • Q: How long should my resume be?
    A: For most UMSI students and new grads, one page is best. More experienced professionals may require two pages.
  • Q: Do I need a different resume for each application?
    A: It’s wise to tailor your resume to the requirements and keywords of each opportunity.
  • Q: Is it okay to include coursework or student projects?
    A: Absolutely! UMSI coursework and projects are valued by employers, especially if they show relevant skills and impact.
  • Q: How often should I update my resume?
    A: Update your resume every semester, after major projects, or whenever you gain a new role or responsibility.
